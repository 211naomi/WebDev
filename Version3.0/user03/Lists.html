How to Water your Plants

  1. Find an appropriate watering can.
  2. Fill your watering can with water from the freshest of springs.
  3. Only bring the gardening essentials, like your watering can, trowel, and clippers. Anything else may be seen as a weapon.
  4. Approach your plants with the utmost reverance (they will not tolerate disrespect).
  5. Bow before your plants and state your intentions.
  6. Allow your plants to inspect you with their roots and vines. If you followed step three, they should let you get closer. If you did not, they will attack without mercy.
  7. Once they've deemed you safe, you may get closer to your plants and begin watering.
  8. Evenly sprinkle your plants with the water from your watering can. Make sure the soil is properly moistened.
  9. Pay attention to your plants, as they will tell you when they've had enough. Stop watering them immediately to avoid retaliation.
  10. Good job! You've successfully watered your plants! Thank them for sparing your life before retreating from your garden.

Plants Good for Beginners (Least Likely to Kill You)

  • Spider Plant
  • Mother-in-Law's Tongue
  • Dracaena Species
  • Succulents and Cacti
