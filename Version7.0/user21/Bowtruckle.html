Bowtruckle Looks, Behavior, and Where to Find Them

  • Looks: The Bowtruckle was a hand-sized, insect-eating, tree dwelling magical beast with two long sharp fingers, brown eyes, and a general appearance of a flat-faced stick figure made of bark and twigs, which served well as camouflage in its natural habitat. A group of Bowtruckles is called a branch.

  • Behavior: It was generally a peaceful creature, but it may attempt to gouge out an attacker's eyes if anything threatened its tree or itself, and is adept at dodging most types of charms. In order to take leaves or wood from a Bowtruckle's tree one would have to offer it wood lice or fairy eggs as a distraction. They don't usually mind someone simply touching their tree, even if they notice the person doing so. A Bowtruckle named Pickett has abandonment/attachment issues we seen this in the film "Fantastic Beast and Where to Find Them".

  • Where to Find Them: The Bowtruckle could be found in western England, southern Germany, and certain Scandinavian forests. A Bowtruckle served as a tree guardian for its home tree, which was usually a tree whose wood was of wand quality (such as a Wiggentree).
