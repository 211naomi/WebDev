Chinese Dragons

Originally Chinese Dragons were a symbol of imperial power in China! They were a representation of the emoriors power and influence. This particular type of dragon is described as long and snake-like with four legs and sharp talons, commonly they are red however they are also often shown as blue.

While some people will tend to forget a creature such as the dragon is a cryptid, almost all mythological creatures and/or animals are technically categorized as cryptids! However, some people don’t think to associate animals like unicorns and centaurs (partial humanoid) with other cryptids because of the negative connotations. Contrary to popular belief not all cryptids originate from horror stories, while some may be creepy and unsettling, most don’t tend to be scary or mean at all.
