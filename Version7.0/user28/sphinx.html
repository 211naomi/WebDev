The Sphinx

At its very core a sphinx is a creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human, with some variation.

You will most often see Sphinxes represented in Egyption culture. They were thought to be great spiritual guardians, often depicted as male with a pharaoh headdress. It is thought that the Great Sphinx of Giza, a giant 4,500-year-old sphinx statue, was built sometime during the reign of Pharaoh Khafre between 2603-2578 B.C.

Another popular edition of the sphinx comes from Greek tradition. This version of the sphinx is depicted as the head of a female on the body of a lion with great big bird wings. The Greek sphinx is known for her complicated riddles, she will give one to any passing travelers she should meet, but those who cannot answer get devoured! This merciless edition of the sphinx commonly appears in fantastical book series’ such as Percy Jackson and the Olypians and Harry Potter.
