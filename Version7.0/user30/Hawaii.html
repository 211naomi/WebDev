 History 

 
Hawaii, constituent state of the United States of America. 
        Hawaii (Hawaiian: Hawai‘i) became the 50th U.S. state on August 21, 1959. 
        Hawaii is a group of volcanic islands in the central Pacific Ocean. 
        The islands lie 2,397 miles (3,857 km) from San Francisco, California, to 
        the east and 5,293 miles (8,516 km) from Manila, in the Philippines, to the west.
         The capital is Honolulu, located on the island of Oahu.
         Hawaii was characterized by Mark Twain as “the loveliest fleet of islands 
         that lies anchored in any ocean.” The name is thought to derive from Hawaiki, 
         the former name of Raiatea, the ancestral home of Polynesians.
